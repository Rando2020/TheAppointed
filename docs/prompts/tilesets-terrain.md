# Tilesets Terrain Prompts

This document contains concise prompt descriptions for generating a tileset of terrain pieces for our tactical RPG.  Each prompt describes a 64×64-pixel tile in a cohesive pixel-art style inspired by classic tactical RPGs but with a darker, more grounded mood akin to *Vagrant Story*.  Prompts should avoid direct references to copyrighted works while capturing the aesthetic.

## Grass Tile
*A lush patch of short, vibrant green grass with subtle shading and tiny tufts to imply texture.  The tile should feel inviting yet grounded, with organic variation across the surface.*

## Road Tile
*A worn cobblestone road tile composed of irregular gray stones with moss growing between the cracks.  The stones should have soft highlights and shadows to convey age and depth.*

## Stone Tile
*A rough-hewn stone floor tile made from large, interlocking granite slabs.  Each slab has chipped edges and faint cracks, and the overall look suggests a weathered castle courtyard.*

## Walls Tile
*A vertical wall section seen in isometric perspective: dark masonry blocks mortared together, with subtle shading to suggest height.  The top edge should be slightly beveled to differentiate it from floor tiles.*

## Water Tile
*A calm pool of deep blue water with gentle ripples and a hint of reflection.  Include faint shoreline details around the edges to make the water blend naturally with adjacent terrain.*

## Shrine Tile
*An ornate shrine platform with carved stone patterns and a small recessed area in the center where a glowing symbol might appear.  The stone should have a warmer, sacred tone compared to other tiles.*

## High Ground Tile
*A raised earthen platform with a visible edge and a slight shadow cast onto lower adjacent tiles.  The top surface is covered in sparse grass and small rocks to reinforce the elevation.*

## Dirt Tile
*A simple dirt patch with varying shades of brown and scattered pebbles.  The surface should look dry and compacted, suitable for paths or barren areas.*

### Surface State Overlays
Apply these as transparent overlays on top of base tiles to indicate environmental conditions.

#### Wet Overlay
*A subtle sheen of water droplets and a faint reflective gloss that makes the underlying tile look damp.*

#### Burning Overlay
*Small animated embers and wisps of smoke drifting upward, with hints of charred edges around the tile.*

#### Frozen Overlay
*A thin layer of frosty crystals spreading across the tile surface, with delicate cracks and a cool bluish tint.*

#### Electrified Overlay
*Sporadic flickers of energy forming tiny lightning bolts that dance across the tile, casting a pale glow.*

### Full Tileset Sheet Prompt
*Assemble all of the above terrain tiles into a single tileset sheet: two rows of four tiles each, organized neatly with consistent spacing.  The sheet should maintain the same pixel-art scale and color palette for seamless integration into the game.  Ensure the overlays are presented separately for compositing.*
