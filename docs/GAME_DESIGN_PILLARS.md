# Vaelthar: Tactical RPG Design Pillars

## One-line vision
A browser-first tactical RPG with Final Fantasy Tactics and Tactics Ogre DNA, built around readable grid tactics, job mastery, elemental terrain reactions, and emotionally charged Guardian-resonance story arcs.

## Pillar 1: Tactical clarity before spectacle
Players should understand movement, range, enemy intent, damage, status risk, and terrain consequences before committing. Big effects are earned only after the tactical choice is clear.

## Pillar 2: Jobs are identity, not just stats
Every job needs a battlefield role, a signature decision pattern, a weakness, a reaction option, and at least one reason to combine it with another job.

## Pillar 3: Terrain is part of the combat system
Water, fire, ice, stormglass, shrine floors, void anchors, elevation, and chokepoints should change decisions. Elemental reactions should affect tiles, not only HP bars.

## Pillar 4: Modern quality-of-life is mandatory
Undo movement, danger previews, battle speed controls, autosave, readable UI, reduced-motion options, and replayable tutorials are baseline features for a 2026 tactics game.

## Pillar 5: Story choices should reshape context
Town states, faction trust, available recruits, shop inventory, side missions, and Guardian resonance outcomes should react to the player’s choices.

## Pillar 6: Prototype vertically
Build one complete playable loop before scaling content: story scene, town, mission board, tactical battle, rewards, world unlock, save/load.

## Do not do yet
- Do not add dozens of jobs before the first grid battle feels good.
- Do not replace the current prototype until tactical systems are validated.
- Do not add copyrighted assets or directly clone FFT/Tactics Ogre naming.
- Do not move to Unreal before the browser tactical loop is fun.
